Building Skills, Bridging Gaps, Creating Opportunities

The Awareness Foundation for Environment and Skill Development is dedicated to empowering marginalized rural communities by providing them with essential skills for sustainable livelihoods. Through targeted training and upskilling programs, we help individuals enhance their employability, bridge the skill gap, and integrate into high-value markets.

Programmes

Basic and advanced training for high level skill generation and improved employability

While the basic training will continue to broaden the skill base, continuous roll out of more advanced training programmes to impart higher skill level are underway to make the target and cohort group more market ready to leverage the higher value chain.

Expose the trainee to the latest design and skills to be able to align with the market needs.

Carry out entrepreneurship courses for the trainees to develop marketing and organisational skills. Upgrade the training centre with more modern techniques and computer based digital education to enable imparting high-level skills.

Establish market linkage to improve earnings and build entrepreneurship

Strategies will be developed to connect the trained cohort with the market and give exposure to the market. The advanced training will focus on promoting self-reliance. Effort will also be made to impact basic soft skills and literacy. Initiative will be taken in organising direct contact programmes to connect the target group with the prospective buyers and suppliers.

Promote community reorganization to enable entrepreneurial growth in the community

Special programmes will be initiated to motivate beneficiaries of the training programme to form self-help groups for more coordinated and synergistic skill sharing, marketing and profit sharing for more asset creation and earnings.

Garments and dress designing

This programme was initially launched in partnership with Naba Niharika, Kolkata(NGO working in the field of empowerment to impart training on cutting, stitching and tailoring. This was started as a level one or basic training programme for women from weaker sections in Mallickpur in South 24 Parganas (South) district of West Bengal, in 2018
